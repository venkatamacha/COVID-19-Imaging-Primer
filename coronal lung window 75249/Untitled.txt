COVID-19 pneumonia
Case contributed by Dr Derek Smith◉
 Diagnosis certain 
Presentation
Recent travel from endemic COVID-19 region. 24 hours confusion with new temperature and desaturation on assessment.

Patient Data
AGE: ~70 years
GENDER: Male
X-RAY
Index CXR
X-ray
Frontal
Supine study. Patchy consolidation in peripheral right midzone.

No pleural abnormality.

X-RAY
Day 3 CXR
X-ray
Frontal
Progressive right mid and lower zone consolidation. Left lung and pleural spaces remains clear.

X-RAY
Worsening oxygenation - day 9 CXR
X-ray
Frontal
Diffuse patchy airspace consolidation in the mid/lower zones bilaterally.

ET tube, right jugular central line and NG tube in situ.

No pleural abnormality.

CT
Day 13 - performed to assess degree of lung injury
Stack Icon
Axial lung window
Stack Icon
Coronal lung window
CT
Axial lung window
Multifocal regions of consolidation and ground-glass opacifications.

These have a peripheral and basal predominance.

No pleural or pericardial effusion.

Case Discussion
Serial imaging demonstrating progressive changes in a patient with PCR confirmed COVID-19 infection.

There can be a rapid deterioration in imaging findings.
